QUTUB MINAR

When it really touches the sky

ABOUT:

The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, India, and is the tallest brick minaret in the world. Built in the 12th century, it is a magnificent example of Indo-Islamic architecture and stands as a symbol of the victory of the Delhi Sultanate.

KEY FEATURES:

  • Height: 72.5 meters (238 feet), tapering from 14.32 m at the base to 2.75 m at the top.
  • Materials: Built with red and buff sandstone.
  • Architecture: Five stories, each with unique balconies and honeycomb design.
  • Inscriptions: Arabic and Nagari script inscriptions telling its history.
