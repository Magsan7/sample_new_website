QUTUB MINAR

When it really touches the sky

ABOUT:
The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, India, and is the tallest brick minaret in the world. Built in the 12th century, it is a magnificent example of Indo-Islamic architecture and stands as a symbol of the victory of the Delhi Sultanate
    

KEY FEATURES:

Height: The tower stands at 72.5 meters (238 feet) tall and has a tapering design, with a base diameter of 14.32 meters shrinking to 2.75 meters at the top.
Materials: It was constructed using red and buff sandstone.
Architecture: The minaret has five stories, each distinguished by a unique, projecting balcony decorated with a "honey-comb" design. The architectural style displays the evolution of design from the Aibak to the Tughlaq dynasties.
Inscriptions: Inscriptions in both Arabic and Nagari scripts on the tower's surface reveal its history and the various repairs it has undergone.
